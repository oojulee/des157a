Best Practices for Modals/Overlays/Dialog Windows

January 30, 2025

Modal windows are elements that sit above the application's main window and are primarily used to capture the user's attention or display additional information. To ensure that these modal windows do not disrupt the user's flow, an appropriate UI is required.

First, an escape hatch should be provided, allowing the user to close the modal at any time. A concise modal title should also be set to enhance user understanding. Buttons should have clear and intuitive labels, such as "Close" or "X," and the background should be darkened to create a focused effect when the modal is open.

Since modals naturally draw the user's attention, it is crucial to design the UI carefully to ensure a seamless and comfortable experience.

Best practices for form design

January 21, 2025

Websites and apps need to be mindful of various design elements to ensure accessibility for a wide range of users. As designers, our goal should be to boldly remove unnecessary content and simplify forms according to priority, thereby increasing user convenience. Eliminating superfluous questions and strategically placing simpler ones first can prevent users from becoming fatigued or overwhelmed by complex inquiries.

When websites or apps require diverse information from users during the subscription process, it's crucial to keep questions as straightforward as possible and focus on essential points to minimize inconvenience. Adjusting the size of each field can prevent confusion caused by unnecessary size imbalances. While many websites and apps use dropdowns, checkboxes are often more efficient when there are fewer items to choose from.

Visual Thinking Strategies Research

Febuary 10, 2025

Websites that rely heavily on images and interactions often engage users primarily through visual content. According to the article "10 Integrating Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills," users can interact with images by asking questions like, "What's going on in this photo?" and by focusing on and commenting directly on the visuals. When used effectively, images can enhance a website's appeal and strengthen its visual identity. Photographs alone can captivate users, but their impact can be amplified with the addition of effects. For example, clicking on an image might trigger new phrases or questions to appear, creating a more interactive experience. However, excessive use of images or using incorrect file types can have negative consequences. To avoid this, it is important to select appropriate image formats and use them thoughtfully to maximize their effectiveness.
