Best practices for form design

January 21, 2025

Websites and apps need to be mindful of various design elements to ensure accessibility for a wide range of users. As designers, our goal should be to boldly remove unnecessary content and simplify forms according to priority, thereby increasing user convenience. Eliminating superfluous questions and strategically placing simpler ones first can prevent users from becoming fatigued or overwhelmed by complex inquiries.

When websites or apps require diverse information from users during the subscription process, it's crucial to keep questions as straightforward as possible and focus on essential points to minimize inconvenience. Adjusting the size of each field can prevent confusion caused by unnecessary size imbalances. While many websites and apps use dropdowns, checkboxes are often more efficient when there are fewer items to choose from.
